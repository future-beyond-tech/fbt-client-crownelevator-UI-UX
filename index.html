A professional report lands on the owner's WhatsApp. The owner approves with a thumb. Every step is tracked, every signature is stored, every dimension flows downstream to manufacturing.

What this is / isn't

What it is: a polished, clickable representation of the five key user journeys — enough for Crown Elevator to see, feel, and sign off on the scope before we build. Every screen shown here becomes a real feature in the shipped platform.

What it isn't (yet): a working backend, a real database, real surveys, real file uploads, or the finished visual design system. Some advanced flows (surveyor check-in, manager dashboards, factory handoff) are out of view. The 3D model is live but intentionally schematic — the production model will be photorealistic with cabin interiors, door types, and material finishes.

Stage 02 · Field capture

A surveyor's morning, made quiet.

The old workflow: paper forms, blurry photos, spec sheets misfiled, quotes rewritten. The new workflow: one app, three minutes, everything structured and timestamped. The capture feeds every downstream system — 3D, report, quote, factory work order.

Why this matters for Crown

Today a quote takes 3–5 days and bounces between surveyor, office, and drafting. With the capture structured at source, the same output is generated in minutes. Mistakes at the quotation stage — wrong shaft dimensions, missed floor, forgotten spec — are caught before they reach the factory floor.

Demo caveat

Fields above are pre-populated with plausible values. In the live product the GPS, camera, and photo upload invoke the device's native permissions; here they're illustrations.
